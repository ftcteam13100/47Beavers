BeaverCamps

The 47 Beavers host summer STEM sessions designed to spark curiosity and build hands-on skills in young learners. Our flagship program, "BeaverCamps," was originally created to help students stay connected and continue learning during and after the COVID-19 pandemic.

These camps cover topics ranging from basic robotics and coding to creative engineering challenges, giving participants a fun and supportive environment to explore STEM.

What Campers Learn

Our camp curriculum is designed by experienced team members and mentors to be engaging, educational, and age-appropriate. Each session introduces new concepts through hands-on projects.

Intro to Robotics

Campers build and control simple robots, learning about motors, sensors, and basic programming logic.

Coding Fundamentals

Using block-based and beginner-friendly tools, campers learn computational thinking and problem-solving skills.

Engineering Challenges

Fun, creative design challenges that teach teamwork, iteration, and the engineering design process.

Camp Information

BeaverCamps are held during the summer months in Montgomery County, Maryland. Sessions are open to students of various ages and experience levels. No prior robotics or coding experience is required.

Interested in signing up or learning more about upcoming sessions? Reach out to us through our contact page.
